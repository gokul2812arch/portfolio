Black Taj
Type of project: Competition Project - Monument Design
Year: 3rd year semester 5
Location: Agra, Delhi
Organizer: Unfuze
Achievement: Shortlisted entry International top 50
As we retell the story of the Taj Mahal and the reflective connection it had with the Black Taj Mahal, we maintain the legend that the emperor Shah Jahan built the Taj Mahal in the memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The vision we conjured across the river is the complete opposite. Marked by neglect and destruction, THE SETTING SUN is the dusk of the Mughal Era, just as the Taj Mahal was the zenith.
Q/A
Q. What Proposition have you chosen for the black TAJ while dealing with the problem given in the brief and how does your design justifies the proposition? E.g. Proposition: Taj was supposed to have a twin.
A. The proposition was to highlight the tragic end. We envisioned a monumental concept across the river. The Mehtab Bagh which had been created solely for the reason to view the majestic charisma of the Taj Mahal from across the river shall now give the spectators the end of the story of the Taj Mahal. The legend states that Black Taj was the reflection of the Taj in the Moonlit Garden. As we retell this legend, we twist it into today‚Äôs lack of ideals and empathy. The reflection of the Taj is a cold hard place that contains Mughal elements, yet ignores the laws and orders behind them.
Q. How your design intervention fits best in the present/modern context while keeping the legacy of TAJ Mahal intact?
A. Our design intervention portrays the modern reality and the fate of the wonders like Taj Mahal under the ignorant attitude of the authorities, ungratefulness and apathy that resounds through the society. These are the reasons that we believe become end of all reigns, including the Mughals. As the visitors look through the Arch of Oblivion, they see the glory of the white marble building through ring of cast iron and concrete bricks and realize just how far we have come from the learnings of the past generations.
Q. What issues have you tackled through your design intervention and how?
A. Through our intervention, we aim to highlight the true ideology behind the construction of the Taj Mahal and the pure brilliance in its symbols and imagery that creates an earthly heaven. The Issue that led our design to its present form was that the Taj Mahal has transcended all association with death. The superimposition of meanings has confirmed the status of Taj with royalty and grandeur that befits the crown of Mughal history but manages to blur the ideology behind its erection. The building which is the greatest memoir of Love, ironically this ideology finds itself on the edge of Oblivion as meanings continue to be superimposed to match various modern products to benefit their own brand names.

A. Our design is a dialogue between the present and the past. We as a team, associated ourselves with various aspects of the Taj Mahal‚Äôs story and have tried to add value through our concept. The factors that we considered were as follows:
The legends and the history surrounding Emperor Shah Jahan‚Äôs life and death. 2) The Modern representation of the Taj Mahal. 3) The reactions of the present generation to the Mughal monument.
Q. What has been the design concept/methodology behind this project?
A. The proposition is elemental in its approach. Featured by the arch that represents the sun descending into the paradisiacal garden of Mehtab Bagh which is punctured by minarets that embody the spirit of Mughal power and grandeur. To justify our concept, it is important to comprehend the symbols behind it.
Emerging from the earth, we achieve a symmetrical and geometrical pattern that focuses on cosmic symbols and sets the stage for the moonlit garden to transition into the dystopian vision of the future. 2) The spectator enters into a grand space embellished by the minarets analogous to a prayer ascending towards heaven. 3) The hour of decadence forces the garden to slowly transform into a vision of destruction where the arch hits the ground that resounds through the elements creating distortion of minarets as we move towards the Arch. 4) The culmination of the experience is the arch that crashes on the landscape like a disaster. It is a shock and a misfit that embodies the unfortunate series of events before Shah Jahan's demise.
Q. What are the functions that has been considered in this program?
A. The landscape functions as an experiential space -
Entering through a jungle of minarets that transition from pristine forms of Taj Mahal to the distorted disfigured forms that transcends into oblivion.
Landscaped through paths, green areas and water pools that juxtapose to produce a cosmic geometry.
Experiencing the Taj Mahal through the arch of the Setting Sun instills a sense of dusk of the Mughal era.
To see the perfection of the Taj through the arch escalates the contrast to the level that intensifies the meaning behind the sepulchral building.
Q. How does this design intervention help the city of Agra? How do you think it adds to the imagination of the people who visit Taj Mahal ‚Äì the touristic destination.
A. On the city level, or more optimistically the national level, we hope to reinstate Taj Mahal as the culmination of the brilliant tradition of funerary Architecture. In the long history of the nation, the building is of no particular significance, it does not commemorate a major victory, nor does it signify a national cathartic experience. It does not embody any lofty principle or institution as other buildings in other countries do. For this reason, the National importance of the Taj Mahal shall not be associated with its patrons who were invaders but with the intensely personal tragedy of one man, at the same time expressing one of the most powerful human impulses "the struggle to stave off Oblivion". As the city that hosts this majestic monument, creating our concept of ‚ÄúThe Setting Sun‚Äù would add to the consciousness of the visitors visiting Agra and awaken curiosity about the past realms of the city.
