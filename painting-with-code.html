Painting with code
A genetic algorithm is used to recreate the image of the dancing building.
100 random triangles are generated with a random color and opacity. and their color shape and location is optimized over a few hundred generations. with a fitness function being the similarity to the original image. The picture starts out as a random mess of overlapping shapes but over the generations the picture starts becoming more and more similar to the original image.

Genetic Algrothim
A genetic algorithm is a search heuristic that is inspired by Charles Darwin’s theory of natural evolution. This algorithm reflects the process of natural selection where the fittest individuals are selected for reproduction in order to produce offspring of the next generation.(1)
The process
I decided to recreate my image with 100 triangles. each triangle would be have a random color, a shape, and size. the full image size was taken as 100 x 100 pixels.
Each triangle in the image was represented by a list of 6 numbers ranging from 0-100 that represented the x,y coordinates of the 3 vertices. and 4 numbers ranging from 0 - 255 that represented the RBG and Alpha (transparency) values. This forms the single gene sequence required. and 100 of these stringed together forms the whole chromosome encapsulating all the details necessary to draw the image.
This takes into account the main generation process of the genetic algorithm. next is the selection process.
The selection function was simple it was just a fitness function that rated each image on its similarity to the initial image by comparing the image pixel by pixel.
As for the reproduction, crossover and the mutation process i left most of the heavy lifting to the DEAP evolutionary computation framework (2)
