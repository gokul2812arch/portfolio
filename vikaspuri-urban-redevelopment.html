Introduction
The redevelopment of Vikaspuri has been a major step towards holistic and cultural sensitive urban planning in this area. It is representative of how, through good design, a settlement can be economically revitalized and functionally improved to serve a greater population and facilitate the area around the community Center. The effort has been to create a landmark development that is inviting, inclusive and accessible for all.

Courtyards and green areas:
Strong public and commercial areas have been created in the courtyard typology that creates a hierarchy of spaces from public to private.
Connecting the greens:
The site is adjacent to two district greens in Vikaspuri, connected seamlessly by dipping the road and creating a green corridor across the road.
Bridge connecting social and commercial areas:
Movement and activity has been devised in a manner to enable the user to experience various kinds of environment while crossing the site moving around the site.
Water body:

Areas for public installations and art were made within the site to enrich the user experience and provide freedom of expression for the public.

The roads have been redesigned to incorporate greens, pedestrian and a bicycle path.

Process
Understanding the precinct
Our understanding of the precinct through various studies has enabled us to make a powerful and informed commentary on the working of the community centre and the nearby residential area. Several problems in traffic, accessibility, aesthetics, maintenance, functionality and gaps in services were identified. At the same time, opportunities such as the effort of the upcoming metro station, the commercial opportunities, and the need for the various new typologies within the mixed-use helped formulate the new development.
SWOT.
Strength: Presence of two large green chunks adjacent to the site. Plenty of schools nearby which could be part of the potential footfall of the centre.
Weakness: The presence of composting pip spilling out on the entire street making the street unusable. Presence of unplanned jhuggis on site, which is catering to the residential areas.
Opportunities: Extra FAR, as the site falls in the TOD influence zone. Providing spaces for informal activities, which at present are infringing the street. but also leading to the vibrancy of the space.
Threats: Community centre at present not providing for residential areas. The proposed metro line might lead to further congestion.
Informing the vision
In redeveloping the Vikaspuri Community centre and adjacent residential zone, we have kept in mind the need to enhance the culture and dynamics of the space by re-imaging the tangible and the intangible form. The design aims to create a people-oriented, sustainable and livable realm within the city.
Structure plan
The intent was to create an urban design that stitched itself seamlessly with the existing fabric. The focus is to maximize all the information we have about the site and respond to those key actors through design.

Climate study
The following climate data has been collected, analyzed, and represented as ground data for built mass analysis and optimization. Various sustainable strategies have been thus employed to reduce heat, maximize daylight and create usable public spaces.
The built mass
The massing on the site was made keeping the following parameters in mind.
1) Sun path, 2) Division of functions (private to public), 3) Pedestrian movement lines and connection nodes from the surrounding, 4) Heights of surrounding building, 5) Pleasing viewpoint to and from the site, 5) Wind movement due to the inherent volumetric shape.
